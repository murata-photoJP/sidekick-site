What Makes Photos Stand Out on 1X.com: Genre-Specific Retouching Trends

Discover the common elements that get photos featured and awarded on 1X.com. Learn genre-specific retouching strategies for portrait, landscape, astro, and monochrome photography.

Marketing Updated: 2026-07-20

Ichiro Murata · Photographer / SideKick Developer

1X.com operates as a curated photography community with a selective review process. While each genre demands its own finishing approach, clear patterns emerge across all accepted work. By examining the trends in portrait, urban landscape, mountain, astrophotography, and monochrome—the platform's major categories—we can identify the universal principles that drive evaluation decisions.

Three Universal Elements of Valued Work

Across all genres, photographs that receive favorable evaluation share these characteristics:

  1. Narrative Strength: Technical precision alone isn't enough. The most praised work communicates a clear intent—viewers understand what the photographer wanted to convey.
  2. Light and Shadow Control: Dodge and burn techniques or tone curves are used to deliberately design how light falls across the frame. This intentional light sculpting is common in valued work.
  3. Unified Color Palette: Each genre has established color preferences, and the most valued work respects these conventions while injecting personal style.

Genre-Specific Retouching Approaches

Portrait Retouching

  • Avoid over-smoothing skin. Use frequency separation and dodge and burn to preserve texture while building dimension and form.
  • Enhance eye impact through catch light emphasis and iris saturation increases.
  • Cinematic color grading—teal and orange palettes are particularly favored—receives consistent praise.
  • De-saturate and dim the background to make the subject stand forward.

Urban Landscape

  • Correcting building verticals and organizing perspective is highly valued.
  • Blue-toned finishes and cool-plus-warm color combinations dominate the category.
  • Atmospheric effects—mist, rain, and reflections—serve as bonus points in evaluation.
  • Both completely depopulated images (emphasizing stillness) and carefully placed single figures (adding narrative) perform well.

Mountain and Alpine Landscape

  • Preserve tonal depth from sky through ridgeline to foreground by lifting shadows while protecting highlights.
  • Stick to cool-to-neutral palettes (blues, greens, grays) with restrained saturation.
  • Golden hour side-lighting and atmospheric perspective created by mist (distant areas pale and soft, foregrounds sharp and detailed) are standard approaches.
  • Place human figures or structures small in the frame to emphasize scale through contrast.

Astrophotography

  • The critical challenge: balance noise reduction against detail preservation. Never sacrifice star grain or Milky Way structure for a cleaner image.
  • Enhance the Milky Way's three-dimensionality through tone curves and levels adjustments, using cool tones (blues and purples) as your foundation.
  • Expose the foreground and star field separately, then blend them so the foreground never falls into pure black.
  • Compose with the horizon line low, allowing the star field to dominate the frame.

Monochrome Photography

  • Build on high contrast: aim for "blacks that grip and whites that glow."
  • Emphasize texture (rock grain, snow surfaces, architectural materials) through high-pass filters or clarity adjustments.
  • Dodge and burn becomes especially critical for creating dimensional form through light and shadow separation.
  • A subtle film grain overlay typically increases perceived polish.

Practical Strategy: Consistency Within Series

When shooting conditions are similar—same camera, lens, and lighting environment—applying identical retouching settings across multiple frames strengthens your series presentation. 1X rewards this visual consistency. If subjects differ meaningfully, simply fine-tune color temperature, exposure, and contrast on a per-image basis while maintaining the overall treatment.

Conclusion

The most valued work on 1X.com follows this formula: respect the genre conventions, control light deliberately, and express your perspective through careful subject selection and color choices. Start by mastering your genre's established preferences, then layer your distinctive voice on top.
